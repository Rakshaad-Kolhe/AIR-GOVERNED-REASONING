# AIR: Adaptive Inference Runtime

**A Governance Layer for Verifying and Constraining Reasoning in Large Language Models**

---

## Overview

Large Language Models (LLMs) demonstrate strong capabilities in generating natural language reasoning but frequently produce logically inconsistent, unsupported, or hallucinated reasoning steps. While techniques like prompt engineering and reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF) improve reasoning quality, they typically operate during training or post-hoc, lacking explicit enforcement of reasoning governance during runtime inference.

**AIR (Adaptive Inference Runtime)** is a modular reasoning governance framework designed to verify, constrain, and trace reasoning generated by language models. Operating as a middleware layer between an LLM's raw output and the final system response, AIR catches and blocks logically invalid inferences before they propagate.

At its core, AIR introduces a structured **Reasoning Intermediate Representation (AIR-IR)** that decomposes natural language reasoning into explicit premises, reasoning operators, and conclusions. This enables reasoning steps to be formally evaluated and governed at runtime.

### The Reasoning Governance Hypothesis
> *Reasoning errors in LLM outputs can be significantly reduced by introducing a runtime governance layer that validates reasoning steps before they are accepted.*

Through rigorous testing on reasoning-trap scenarios, AIR successfully rejected 100% of invalid reasoning attempts, bringing the **Invalid Reasoning Acceptance Rate (IRAR)** down to **0.0** (compared to the baseline LLM's 1.0 IRAR).

---

## Key Features and Contributions

- **AIR-IR (Adaptive Inference Representation):** A structured intermediate representation converting unstructured text into formal reasoning steps ($S = \langle Premises, Operator, Conclusion, Evidence \rangle$).
- **Runtime Governance Architecture:** Deterministic verification of reasoning using explicit governance rules before the system produces an output.
- **Constraint Learning Mechanism:** The system dynamically extracts and generalizes reusable reasoning constraints from past logical failures, enabling earlier and faster rejection of flawed reasoning patterns.
- **Reasoning Trace Graph:** Full observability into the reasoning path, documenting why specific steps were accepted or rejected.
- **Admissibility Checking:** Upfront filtering of logically flawed architectures, seamlessly interacting with the constraint library pipeline.

---

## System Architecture

The pipeline of the AIR middleware naturally follows this sequence:

```text
User Query
   ↓
LLM Reasoning Generator  (Raw text generation)
   ↓
Reasoning Extractor      (Detects premises, rules, conclusions)
   ↓
AIR-IR Builder           (Constructs formalized AIR-IR steps)
   ↓
AIR Executor             (Validates steps, checks constraints, admissibility)
   ↓
Constraint Learning      (Compiles generalized constraints from rejections)
   ↓
Trace Graph              (Records the full causal reasoning trace)
   ↓
Verified Output          (Accepted reasoning or safe suspension)
```

## Codebase Structure

The AIR project is highly modular. Here is a comprehensive breakdown of the project folders:

### 1. Structural & Representation Layer
- **`air/`**: Contains the core graphical representation primitives such as nodes, edges, and the reasoning graph structure itself.
- **`air_ir/`**: Handles the **Reasoning Intermediate Representation**. It houses the `AIRIRBuilder` to compile raw structures into `AIRStep` objects, and the `AIRExecutor` which orchestrates the execution and governance of these steps.
- **`air_trace/`**: Responsible for the reasoning observability graph, keeping a secure ledger of what steps were taken and why they were accepted or rejected.

### 2. Runtime & Governance Engine
- **`air_runtime/`**: Houses the main runtime loops, the `ReasoningSupervisor`, the `FailureDetector`, and the `RepairEngine` which coordinates the retry mechanics.
- **`governance/`**: Contains the semantic rule engine (`EALEngine`), conflict resolution, exception handling, rule prioritization, and validation of evidence strength (Pramana).
- **`air_kernel/`**: The core integration mechanism that ties together constraint solving, policy checking, and the EAL evaluation engine.

### 3. Safety, Policy & Constraints
- **`air_constraints/`**: A critical module that powers the system's learning and safety barriers. Contains the `ConstraintLibrary`, the `AdmissibilityChecker` for upfront logic-flaw detection, and the `ConstraintExtractor`/`ConstraintGeneralizer` for dynamically learning rule bounds.
- **`air_policy/`**: Policy engine and rule definitions governing rigid domain policies (like data privacy violations).

### 4. Language Interfaces & Extraction
- **`llm/`**: Interface wrappers and prompt templates for querying the underlying Large Language Model.
- **`extractor/`**: Performs the Natural Language Processing tasks of partitioning raw LLM text into discrete `PremiseDetector`, `RuleDetector`, and `ConclusionDetector` outputs.
- **`knowledge/`**: The `KnowledgeBase`, `FactStore`, and evidence management systems. Supplies the grounded foundational context needed to validate whether reasoning predicates are actually currently applicable.

### 5. Experimentation & Evaluation
- **`air_experiments/`**: Contains the rigorous testing infrastructure, scenario runners, and output result JSONs tracking the mathematical learning curve of the system.
- **`air_evaluation/`** & **`evaluation/`**: Additional scripts and utilities for measuring model reasoning performance and Invalid Reasoning Acceptance Rate (IRAR).

### 6. Subsystem Integrations 
- **`api/`**, **`air_middleware/`**: Integration folders allowing AIR to be mounted as an API service or directly into an existing backend pipeline.
- **`demos/`**, **`rules/`**, **`tests/`**: Demonstration scripts, domain-specific rule definitions, and unit testing coverage respectively.

## Experimental Evaluation & Constraint Learning

To evaluate AIR, we utilize 10 reasoning trap scenarios representing common logical failures (e.g., Circular Reasoning, Missing Evidence, Invalid Rule Application, Contradictory Premises). 

When tested over 300 executions:
- **Baseline (Ungoverned LLM):** Accepted 100% of the reasoning traps directly (IRAR: 1.0).
- **AIR System:** Rejected 100% of the reasoning traps safely (IRAR: 0.0).

### Active Constraint Learning Curve
Furthermore, AIR features a fully functioning automated learning curve. 
1. **Run 1:** The `AdmissibilityChecker` rejects the logically flawed reasoning step, tagging it as `inadmissible_reasoning`. The constraint system dynamically compiles this rejection into a generalized behavioral constraint.
2. **Future Runs:** Flawed reasoning steps matching the learned pattern do not even reach deeper evaluation; they are instantly and cheaply intercepted by the `ConstraintLibrary` triggered filter.

## Limitations and Future Work
Current limitations include reliance on straightforward constraint generalization and testing against deterministic reasoning traps. 

Future initiatives will focus on:
- Scaling evaluation across massive reasoning datasets.
- Integrating more advanced, automated constraint discovery features.
- Directly plugging AIR into wider AI safety and compliance frameworks.
- Expanding learning-based governance mechanics beyond explicit logic rules.
